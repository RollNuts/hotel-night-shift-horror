# STEAM RELEASE STRATEGY

Status: active for the hotel night-shift horror product.

## Codex Decision Frame

- Build: a Steam-first, offline, buy-to-own release path.
- Do not build: public promises, demos, screenshots, or trailers with placeholders or unclear assets.
- Market reason: the store page must immediately communicate "old hotel night shift, routine decisions, things go wrong."
- Unreal reason: all store media should come from real Unreal gameplay capture.
- Solo-dev/low-cost reason: no server operations, no live events, no account support.
- Art/audio bar: store assets must show final-intent lighting, sound direction, UI restraint, and hotel identity.
- No-small-room bar: no test-room screenshots or debug clips in marketing.
- Steam quality bar: five screenshots, a strong trailer opening, stable settings/save/quit, and honest playtime.
- Veripsa unit: track screenshots, trailer beats, store copy, build branches, review risks, and asset rights.

## Store Promise

The store page must answer in seconds:

- You work the night shift at an old business hotel.
- You answer calls, watch cameras, patrol floors, make door and guest decisions, and report incidents.
- The game is a complete single-night horror experience.
- Your judgment creates endings and player stories.

## Screenshot Requirements

At least five real gameplay screenshots:

- Front desk with phone/work tools.
- Security monitor or surveillance view.
- Hallway or guest-room door decision.
- Back-of-house or stair/elevator route.
- Abnormal event that shows fear without spoiling the whole game.

No screenshot may contain:

- Placeholder props/UI/lighting.
- Debug overlays.
- Unlicensed assets.
- Unadapted asset-pack look.

## Trailer Opening

The first 15 seconds should show:

- Night shift role.
- Phone or request.
- Camera/log check.
- Walk through hotel space.
- Door/guest decision.
- A clear abnormal escalation.

Do not lead with long logos, lore text, or non-gameplay mood shots.

## Demo Policy

A demo is allowed only when:

- The first 5 minutes teach work and abnormal rules clearly.
- No public-facing placeholders remain.
- The demo ends with a reason to wishlist.
- Save/settings/restart/quit work.
- Audio and lighting are final-intent.

## Release Gates

The game cannot release until:

- First 30 seconds explain controls and work.
- First 5 minutes explain the abnormal pressure.
- The player wants to answer the next phone call.
- Each playthrough creates explainable judgment mistakes.
- UI does not obstruct fear or work.
- Bugs do not turn fear into comedy.
- Save/load/settings/quit are stable.
- Mid-range PC and Steam Deck-like readability/performance are considered.
- All public assets are licensed.
- No visible placeholders remain.
