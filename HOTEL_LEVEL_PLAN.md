# HOTEL LEVEL PLAN

Status: initial production-level plan.

The hotel must be small, dense, readable, and reusable. It is not an open world and not a maze.

## Required Areas

| Area | Product Role | Screenshot/Trailer Job |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Front desk | Main work hub: phone, guest admission/refusal, log/report, keys/tools | Immediately communicates the night-shift job |
| Surveillance point | Monitor checks and uncertainty before leaving the desk | Shows work plus fear in one image |
| Lobby | Public threshold between outside guests and hotel interior | Supports admit/refuse decisions |
| Elevator | Controlled vertical movement, sound cue, risk transition | Creates audible dread and pacing |
| Emergency stairs | Alternate route, escape, shortcut, and sound-led fear | Supports chase/flee without large systems |
| Guest floor hallway | Door decisions, knocks, patrol, camera mismatch | Main horror corridor |
| Guest-room doorway | Open/refuse/close decision point | Core screenshot/trailer beat |
| Back of house | Staff-only route, breaker/storage/logical shortcuts | Gives hotel credibility and tension |
| Locked/blocked route | Simple sealing/shortcut logic | Supports single-night progression without sprawl |

## Layout Principles

- The player should learn the hotel by work repetition, not map screens.
- Front desk to first guest door must be short enough for repeated calls but long enough for dread.
- Elevator and stairs must have distinct sound identities.
- Hallways must support sightline staging, not random length.
- Locked/blocked routes should create meaningful choices, not confusion.
- Environmental clues should tell situation, not exposition.

## Required Fear Points

- Phone rings from the desk while the player is away.
- Monitor shows a hallway state that may not match reality.
- Elevator movement is heard before seen.
- Door sound happens before the player knows which door.
- Stairwell ambience creates uncertainty about vertical position.
- Back-of-house lighting or machinery changes the mood without a new subsystem.

## First Production Slice Boundary

The first slice uses only:

- Front desk.
- Surveillance point.
- Lobby edge.
- One elevator or stair route.
- One guest-floor hallway segment.
- One guest-room door.
- One report/log return.

No extra floors, no basement expansion, no chase route, and no additional guest-room content until this loop is proven with light, sound, UI, performance, and capture evidence.
