# FIRST PRODUCT SLICE

Status: approved next implementation target.

## Slice Promise

The player receives a night-shift request at the front desk, checks surveillance or the report log, walks to one guest-room door, decides whether to open/refuse/close, returns or records the result, and experiences one abnormal variation that makes the same loop frightening.

## Player Flow

1. Start at front desk.
2. Phone rings.
3. Player answers.
4. Request/incident is presented through phone audio and minimal work UI.
5. Player checks monitor/log for context.
6. Player leaves desk.
7. Player reaches elevator/stair transition.
8. Player enters guest-floor hallway.
9. Player approaches the target door.
10. Player decides to open, refuse, or close/leave.
11. Player records/reports the outcome.
12. One abnormal variation changes what the player thought they understood.

## Required Quality Axes

- Gameplay: one complete work loop.
- Visual: old business hotel mood in every shot.
- Camera: first-person readability and interactable framing.
- Animation: phone/door/report feedback or equivalent motion.
- VFX: only if needed for the abnormal variation.
- SFX: phone, receiver, monitor, footsteps, elevator/stair, hallway, door, report.
- Music/ambience: lobby, hallway, and transition ambience.
- UI feedback: minimal and work-equipment flavored.
- Level context: production hotel areas, not a test room.
- Performance: baseline capture.
- Capture readiness: one screenshot and one 15-second clip candidate.

## Out Of Scope

- Multiple guest floors.
- Combat.
- Inventory beyond work tools.
- Complex NPC conversation.
- Online features.
- Randomized hotel generation.
- Full ending system.
- Store page publication.
